Caesar crosses the Rubicon as Rome braces itself for Civil War

By Titus Livius

Wed 22 Jan 2020 12:00 GMT

Julius Caesar has led a single legion, Legio XIII, south over the Rubicon from Cisalpine Gaul to Italy, making his way to Rome. In doing so, he has deliberately broken the law on imperium and made armed conflict inevitable. Sources have described Caesar as undecided as he approached the river, and attribute the crossing to a supernatural apparition. It was reported that Caesar dined with Sallust, Hirtius, Oppius, Lucius Balbus and Sulpicus Rufus on the night after the crossing into Italy on January 10. Legal commentators note that imperium is forbidden by the law as a capital offence. Furthermore, obeying the commands of a general who does not legally possess imperium is a capital offence. If a general enters Italy in command of an army, both the general and his soldiers become outlaws and are automatically condemned to death. Generals are thus obliged to disband their armies before entering Italy.
