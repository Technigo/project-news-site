No More Capybara Hunting in Colombia

... or almost. Colombian law enforcement arrested an illegal capybara hunting network in the region of Casanare. The organization was not only hunting these adorable hamster-like creatures, but they were also selling their meat in awful sanitary conditions! Their arrest is part of a recent initiative for the conservation of the species in the South American country.

Capybaras (or chiguiros, as they are called in Colombia) are a protected species under Colombian law. Sport hunting was banned decades ago, and commercial hunting is only allowed for three months a year. Obtaining a license to hunt and sell capybara isn't easy, either! So it's good to hear that authorities are making sure people are actually leaving chiguiros alone. Long live the world's biggest rodent!
